Overview

Executive Summary

BALTIKA operates a large (3,438-product) Eastern European grocery store targeting Russian-speaking diaspora in Spain. While the business has real infrastructure — next-day delivery, cash-on-delivery, a loyalty programme, and coverage across all Spanish provinces — the website scores the lowest in the competitive set on every dimension. The site is Russian-language only, invisible to Spanish-language search, has no visual brand identity, and presents zero social proof. Every competitor has addressed these gaps. The opportunity is significant: a new site could leapfrog Magnit and Cesta Selecta with focused execution on Spanish SEO, a warm visual identity, and an emotional "eat like at home" narrative backed by the genuine logistics advantage BALTIKA already has.

Winning Formula

Patterns of the Top 10%

5 things every winning site does that BALTIKA currently doesn't.

1

Spanish-Language Interface

Every competitor operates primarily in Spanish. This unlocks Google search, serves second-generation immigrants, Spanish partners of diaspora members, and food explorers. BALTIKA's Russian-only UI returns zero Spanish search results.

2

Emotional "Eat Like at Home" Narrative

Top sites lead with an emotional hook before any product grid. Cesta Selecta: "Las marcas más famosas de tu país para que comas como en casa." This is the emotional unlock that converts a price-shopper into a loyal customer.

3

Visible Social Proof on Homepage

Cesta Selecta has 3 named testimonials with photos. Balkanica shows €27M annual revenue. Magnit shows "En stock" confidence badges. Trust is established in the first scroll. BALTIKA has zero social proof anywhere on the site.

4

Free Shipping Threshold as Hero Message

Every competitor makes their free shipping threshold impossible to miss — header banners, hero text, repeated in cart. BALTIKA buries this on a separate /dostavka page. It's the single highest-converting message being wasted.

5

Intentional Visual Brand Identity

Magnit's warm cream + crimson. Cesta Selecta's forest green + olive. These colors aren't random — they evoke warmth, food, and home. BALTIKA's electric blues feel mismatched and cold. No custom fonts, no photography direction, no visual story.

Typography

Heading: Playfair Display — elegant serif used by premium food brands globally. Commands trust, warmth, heritage.
Body: Inter — the cleanest neutral sans. Used by Stripe, Linear, Notion. Invisible but professional.
Justified by: Magnit uses Nunito Sans (rounded, modern). We go one step more premium with a serif heading. Cesta Selecta uses Barlow — good but not distinctive enough.

Photography Style

Close-up, warm food photography. Glistening herring. Dark rye bread with butter. Amber honey in a jar. Steam from pelmeni. Condensation on a cold glass of kvas. Warm, nostalgic, appetizing. Shot on a natural wood or linen surface — never clinical white.

3 Headline Options

Based on headline formulas used by top competitors:

  1. "Los sabores de casa, entregados mañana." (The tastes of home, delivered tomorrow.)
  2. "Tu tienda de productos del Este. Toda España en 24h."
  3. "Lo que no encuentras en el super, lo encontrarás aquí."
